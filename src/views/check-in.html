ONBOARD
To secure a spot on regular sailings, it's advisable to reserve ahead and check-in online. Boarding passes can be obtained and scanned from your mobile device. If that fails, an ID can be presented for a printed pass at the terminal.
Passengers may bring their own luggage for free on regular sailings, but it's limited to what they can carry or drag. Small personal items are allowed onboard, but all other luggage must be checked into the cargo hold with barcoded tags assigned to the itinerary ID. Luggage can be collected shortly after docking, and tags can be reused for the return trip on round-trip itineraries.
Caribbean Sprinter's vessels have US Coast Guard-approved safety equipment, tested regularly for effectiveness. The water depth en route is between 3 and 4 feet outside the channel, and the temperature is warm. There have been no reports of a vessel sinking or passenger submersion with us.
Teating meals, consuming large amounts of alcohol, or smoking combustion cigarettes is not allowed onboard during regularly scheduled sailings. If you wish to vape while onboard, we encourage you to sit on the upper deck, in the open air.
Caribbean Sprinter allows service animals to travel with those they assist, and you can contact customer service for questions about traveling with your pet. We'll find a sailing with fewer guests, usually an early morning one from the islands and mid-afternoon sailings from Belize City, for the comfort of your pet and other passengers. We also offer doggy life vests and soft muzzles. Important: If a dog becomes out of control, the Captain may decide to confine it in a crate.
Caribbean Sprinter sanitizes vessels between sailings, even though the U.S. government declared the end of the COVID-19 pandemic. Masks are optional on the terminal premises and onboard the vessels, and bandanas can be used as face coverings but should be researched to avoid gang references.
